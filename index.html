Methodology

Gen-L^2 is an inference strategy for high-quality long video generation with pretrained short video diffusion models, comprised of two main components: quadratic temporal expansion and temporal consistent noise scheduler. For the quadratic temporal expansion, we quadratically extend the video length through joint approximation with feature-level temporal expansion and noise-level temporal expansion. For the temporal consistent noise scheduler, we first introduce noise merge initialization strategy that eases the burden of aligning long frames. Additionally, we propose noise travel that we periodically repeat the noising and denoising process during inference, which allows for better information transfer between frames and alleviates potential denoising conflicts, improving the quality of generated results.
